What is Supply Chain Management?

It's how the world
runs — literally.

Supply Chain Management is the discipline of getting things from where they're made to where they're needed. Efficiently. On time. At scale.

SCM professionals design the systems that coordinate suppliers, factories, shipping, and last-mile delivery across global networks. When supply chains work well, the world runs smoothly. When they don't, shelves go empty and prices spike.

"Supply chain management is how business gets done — and every industry in the world depends on getting it right."
Prof. William McLaury, SCM Program Director, RBS

⏰ When Should I Switch?

The best time is as a freshman or sophomore — maximum flexibility, minimal credit loss. But juniors can absolutely add SCM as a double major.

  • Freshman: Full flexibility — all pathways open
  • Sophomore: Still easy — most credits count
  • Junior: Double major is the recommended path
  • Senior: Speak with an advisor — options may exist

📋 Which Credits Transfer?

All RBS core courses count. Certain electives from your current major may double-count via the shared elective policy.

  • All RBS core courses: count in full
  • Finance: Treasury Management may double-count
  • Marketing: Marketing Research may double-count
  • BAIT: Fundamentals of SCM with SAP may double-count
  • Management: Negotiations may double-count
